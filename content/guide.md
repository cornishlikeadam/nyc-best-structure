## SECTION I: THE GRID

**Why New York is Built Different**

Manhattan's street grid was laid in 1811 — the Commissioners' Plan — a bet that the city would grow north long before anyone lived above Houston Street. That's the energy here: build the infrastructure before the demand exists. The grid isn't just navigation. It's a philosophy.

**Key Coordinates:**

- **14th Street** — The dividing line. Below it the streets lose their numbers and the city loses its predictability. That's the point.
- **125th Street** — Harlem's main artery. The Apollo is here but so is everything else — the vendors, the churches, the Dominican food spots that don't need your review.
- **Canal Street** — Where Chinatown, Tribeca, and SoHo collide. The smell of fish, the sound of haggling, the $800 handbag next to the $8 one. Possibly the same bag.

## SECTION II: EAT

**The Non-Negotiables**

1. **Joe's Pizza, Carmine St.** — No explanation needed. If you ask for a fork they will remember your face.
2. **Russ & Daughters, Houston St.** — Lox, cream cheese, everything bagel. Operating since 1914. Your favorite brunch spot could never.
3. **Los Tacos No. 1, Chelsea Market** — The adobada taco is a spiritual experience. Cash speeds things up.
4. **Lucali, Carroll Gardens** — BYOB pizza in Brooklyn. The wait is long. The pizza is why you waited.
5. **Halal Guys, 53rd & 6th** — The white sauce is the real currency of Midtown.
6. **Xi'an Famous Foods** — Hand-pulled noodles. The spicy cumin lamb. Multiple locations, all correct.
7. **Peter Luger, Williamsburg** — Cash only. Porterhouse for two. Been here since 1887. Don't order the menu — order the steak.
8. **Dimes, Chinatown/LES** — Healthy-adjacent. The crowd is beautiful. The grain bowl is too.

**Street Rules:**
- Dollar pizza is a genre, not a price point. $1.50 is still dollar pizza.
- If there's no line, question it.
- Bodegas are restaurants. Act accordingly.

## SECTION III: MOVE

**Getting Around Without Looking Lost**

- **Subway**: The MTA is chaos with a schedule. Download the app but trust the locals on the platform more. Express vs. local will humble you at least once.
- **Citi Bike**: Docks are everywhere below 96th. The electric ones are worth the upcharge. Return it before the 45-minute mark or pay.
- **Walking**: This is the real transit. You'll cover 5–8 miles a day without trying. Wear real shoes.
- **Ferry**: NYC Ferry from Brooklyn to Manhattan is $4 and has better views than any rooftop bar. Rockaway Beach run in summer is mandatory.
- **Cab Rule**: If the roof light center panel is lit, it's available. If the side lights are on, it's off duty. If nothing's lit, it's taken. Now you know.

## SECTION IV: SEE

**Architecture & Vantage Points**

- **The Woolworth Building** — Neo-Gothic. 1913. They called it the Cathedral of Commerce and they were right.
- **Vessel, Hudson Yards** — Love it or hate it, walk the stairs. 154 flights. The structure is the point.
- **Brooklyn Bridge at dawn** — Tourist trap by noon. Sacred ground at 6 AM. Walk from Brooklyn to Manhattan, not the other way.
- **The High Line** — An elevated rail turned park. Walk it from Gansevoort to 34th St. The Whitney Museum is at the south entrance.
- **Top of the Rock vs. Empire State** — Top of the Rock: you see the Empire State in your photo. Empire State: you're inside it and can't see it. Choose accordingly.
- **Green-Wood Cemetery, Brooklyn** — 478 acres. Basquiat is buried here. So is Boss Tweed. The views of the harbor rival any observation deck.

## SECTION V: BUY

**Where to Spend (and Where Not To)**

- **Strand Bookstore, Broadway & 12th** — 18 miles of books. The rare book room upstairs is where the real ones shop.
- **Canal Street Market** — Curated vendors. Good for gifts that don't look like gifts from New York.
- **Brooklyn Flea, Dumbo/Williamsburg** — Vintage, design objects, random excellence. Weekends only.
- **Kith, SoHo** — Streetwear temple. The cereal bar inside is not ironic — it's good.
- **McNally Jackson Books** — Independent. The risograph printing station in the back is usable by anyone.
- **Artists & Fleas, Chelsea Market/Williamsburg** — Local makers. Jewelry, prints, leather goods. Rotating vendors keep it fresh.

**Buying Rule:** If you're in Times Square and about to buy something, don't.

## SECTION VII: KNOW

**Unwritten Rules**

1. Stand on the right side of the escalator. Walk on the left.
2. Don't stop in the middle of the sidewalk. Pull over like a car.
3. Eye contact on the subway is a choice. Make it wisely.
4. Tipping: 20% minimum. This isn't optional. Servers make $2/hour base.
5. The "showtime" dancers on the L train are talented. Keep your face clear.
6. Jaywalking is a birthright. But look both ways — bike lanes are silent killers.
7. If someone asks for directions, help them. You live here now.
8. Rats are neighbors. Pigeons are coworkers. Accept it.
9. The bodega cat is not a pet. It's management.
10. If a place has been open for 40+ years, eat there before it's gone.
